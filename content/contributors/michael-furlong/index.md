Dr. Michael Furlong is a research officer at the National Research Council of Canada / University of Waterloo Collaboration Centre, where he works on probabilistic models of neural computation. Michael has worked on automating planetary exploration, software architecture for Lunar rovers, autonomous flood mapping, neuromorphic computing for radiation environments, as well as field work, using uncrewed underwater, aerial, and ground vehicles. He holds a Ph.D. and M.S. in Robotics from Carnegie Mellon University, an M.Sc. in Neuroscience from Oxford, and a B.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Memorial University.
